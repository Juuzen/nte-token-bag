# Product

## Register

product

## Users

TTRPG players (player role) and narrators (GM role) using the app during active *Not the End* sessions — at the table or remote. They are deep in a narrative moment; the app is a mechanical support layer, not the focus. Cognitive load is high; attention is on the story, not the screen.

## Product Purpose

A real-time token bag simulator for the *Not the End* TTRPG system. The narrator loads and manages the bag; players request draws; the bag resolves outcomes (positive, negative, or random). The app replaces a physical bag of tokens during play, with a shared state across the table.

Success looks like: a draw happens in seconds, the result is unmistakable at a glance, and the session continues without the tool intruding.

## Brand Personality

Atmospheric · Immersive · Minimal

The app should feel like it belongs in the world of NtE — dystopian near-future, tense, alive. Not a utility bolted onto the session; a presence that amplifies the mood of the moment. When nothing is happening, it fades. When a draw fires, it holds attention briefly, then steps back.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / dashboard**: No clean white-card layouts, metric-grid patterns, sidebar navs, or enterprise UX conventions. Nothing that reads as a productivity tool.
- **Fantasy TTRPG aesthetics**: No parchment, gothic fonts, or medieval ornament. NtE is a near-future sci-fi world — the aesthetic should be cold, dark, and precise.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the session.** The UI earns silence. Every element that doesn't need attention should not demand it.
2. **Every draw feels consequential.** Motion and visual feedback should match the weight of the moment — the result of a draw should be unmistakable without being loud.
3. **Role-specific surface.** Players see what players need; narrators see what narrators need. Don't show both roles everything simultaneously.
4. **Aesthetic belongs to the world.** Dark, cold, tense. Not generic sci-fi chrome — NtE's specific near-future dread. The UI is part of the fiction.
5. **Trust the participants.** No over-explanation, no nagging confirmations, no tutorial scaffolding. The people at this table know what they're doing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort — no hard compliance requirement. Target reasonable contrast for dark-room play (screens in dim lighting). Support reduced-motion where animations are present. Keyboard navigation for narrator controls where feasible.
